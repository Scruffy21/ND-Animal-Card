Eurasian Lynx

Like domestic cats, lynx will "chatter" at prey that is just out of reach. According to one theory, it is to lull its alertness before they can creep up closer.

  • Habitat: High altitude forests
  • Food: Small mammals, deer, birds, fish
  • Lifespan: Up to 21 years in captivity
  • Conservation Status: Least concern
  • Estimated Population: 22,000 in Russia and 5,000 in Europe
  • Average Weight: 18-30kg for males; 8-21kg for females

Reddish or brown coated in summer, in winter this is replaced by thick silver-grey to greyish-brown fur. Has relatively long legs, with large furred paws that act like snowshoes. It also possesses a short tail with an all-black tip. Became extirpated by the middle of the 19th century in most countries of Central and Western Europe. Subsequently successfully reintroduced into the wild in some of those areas.
